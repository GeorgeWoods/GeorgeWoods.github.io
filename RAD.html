Rapid Application Development

Information about the Rapid Application Development (RAD):

  • The Spiral Model was documented in a paper by Barry W. Boehm in 1988.
  • The spiral model adds the RAD prototyping and risk analysis to the waterfall model and splits the entire cycle into four quadrants
  • Four quadrants dividing stages
  • Quadrant 1 – Determine objectives, alternatives and constraints.
  • Quadrant 2 – Evaluate alternatives, risks are identified and resolved. If risks are too high, the project may be stopped. A prototype is created.
  • Quadrant 3 – The prototype is developed.
  • Quadrant 4 – Planning takes place for the next phase.
